# [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com/?via=outreachly) vs [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com/?via=outreachly) for Freelancers: Which Email Marketing Platform Is Right for Your Business?

As a freelancer or small business consultant, you're juggling multiple responsibilities—client acquisition, service delivery, and business growth. Your email marketing platform needs to work as hard as you do without draining your budget or requiring a technical team to operate it.

The challenge? Finding the right tool that balances affordability, ease of use, and powerful features specifically tailored for solopreneurs and small agencies.

Two platforms consistently top the recommendation list: Brevo and HubSpot. But which one actually delivers the best value for freelancers? Let's break it down.

## Pricing: The Budget Reality Check

For most freelancers, cost is the primary decision factor. Here's where Brevo and HubSpot differ significantly.

**Brevo** offers a genuinely free plan with unlimited contacts—you pay based on email volume rather than subscriber count. Their paid plans start at around €20/month, making it accessible for solopreneurs just starting out.

**HubSpot** also has a free tier, but it's more limited. Their free plan caps you at 300 contacts, which many growing freelancers quickly outgrow. Paid plans start at $45/month, which can feel steep when you're managing cash flow carefully.

If you're bootstrapping your business, Brevo's pricing structure is more forgiving during the early growth phase.

## Feature Comparison: What You Actually Need

| Feature | Brevo | HubSpot |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Unlimited contacts (free) | ✅ Yes | ❌ Limited to 300 |
| Email automation | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| CRM included | ✅ Yes (basic) | ✅ Yes (more advanced) |
| Landing pages | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| SMS marketing | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes (paid plan) |
| Ease of use | ✅ Excellent | ⚠️ Steep learning curve |
| Customer support | ⚠️ Varies | ✅ Excellent |

Both platforms include email marketing, CRM, and automation—the core tools freelancers need. However, the user experience differs considerably.

## Ease of Use: Time Is Money

As a freelancer, you need tools that work intuitively. You don't have time to watch dozens of tutorials or troubleshoot complex workflows.

**Brevo's advantage:** The interface is straightforward and intuitive. Setting up your first automation sequence takes minutes, not hours. The dashboard clearly shows what you need to see, without overwhelming you with unnecessary features.

**HubSpot's challenge:** It's powerful but complex. The platform has a steeper learning curve, especially for beginners. You'll likely spend your first week exploring features you don't immediately need. That said, once you master it, HubSpot offers more sophisticated options for scaling.

For solopreneurs who want to launch campaigns quickly, Brevo wins on accessibility.

## Automation and Workflows: Growing Beyond Manual Work

Email automation is essential for freelancers who want to scale without increasing their workload proportionally.

**Brevo** provides solid automation capabilities with trigger-based workflows. You can set up sequences for welcome emails, follow-ups after consultations, and nurture campaigns for leads. It's enough for most freelancers to eliminate repetitive manual tasks.

**HubSpot's advantage:** The automation features are more granular and sophisticated. You can create complex, multi-step workflows with conditional logic that adapts to subscriber behavior. If your business grows and you need advanced segmentation or behavior-triggered sequences, HubSpot delivers.

However, for a coach or consultant handling 100-500 active leads, Brevo's automation tools are entirely adequate and less overwhelming to manage.

## Customer Support and Learning Resources

When something breaks or you're stuck, responsive support matters.

**HubSpot** excels here. Their support team is responsive, and their educational resources (Academy courses, documentation, community forums) are extensive. This investment in user success is reflected in their pricing.

**Brevo's support** is adequate but less comprehensive. Response times can vary, and the knowledge base isn't as detailed. However, for straightforward questions and common scenarios, you'll typically find answers.

## Which Platform Fits Your Needs?

**Choose Brevo if you:**
- Are just starting out or bootstrapping
- Want unlimited contacts on a free plan
- Value simplicity and quick setup over advanced features
- Have a tight budget and need to justify every expense
- Manage a smaller audience (under 1,000 regular contacts)

**Choose HubSpot if you:**
- Can invest in a platform with higher upfront costs
- Want comprehensive support and extensive training resources
- Plan to scale significantly and need advanced automation
- Prefer an all-in-one ecosystem (CRM, sales, marketing, service)
- Value having a clear growth path built into the platform

## The Bottom Line

For most freelancers, consultants, and small business coaches, **Brevo is the smarter choice**. It offers the essential tools you need to build relationships with clients and automate repetitive tasks—all without unnecessary complexity or budget strain.

You can start for free with unlimited contacts and grow into their paid plans as your business scales. The time you save by not wrestling with a complicated interface is time you can spend serving clients or growing your business.

Save HubSpot for when your organization reaches the point where advanced automation and comprehensive support become essential investments for managing growth.

Start with Brevo, master it, and upgrade only when your business genuinely requires more sophisticated tools.
