# [Monday.com](https://monday.com/?via=outreachly) vs [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/?via=outreachly) for Coaches: Which Tool Should You Choose?

If you're running a coaching practice, you're juggling multiple responsibilities—client management, communication, scheduling, and marketing. The question isn't whether you need project management and email marketing tools; it's which ones will actually save you time and money without overwhelming you with complexity.

Many coaches face a critical decision: should you invest in Monday.com for workflow management or Mailchimp for email marketing? Better yet, do you need both? Let's break down these tools so you can make an informed choice for your specific coaching business.

## Understanding Each Tool's Core Purpose

**Monday.com** is a visual project management platform designed to organize tasks, deadlines, and team workflows. It's built for coaches who work with clients and need to track progress, manage multiple projects, and collaborate with team members or contractors.

**Mailchimp** is an email marketing platform that helps you build subscriber lists, create automated campaigns, and track engagement metrics. It's perfect for coaches who want to nurture leads, send newsletters, and build relationships with past and prospective clients.

The key insight: these tools serve different purposes. Monday.com manages *how* you organize your work internally, while Mailchimp manages *how* you communicate externally with your audience.

## Core Features Comparison

| Feature | Monday.com | Mailchimp |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Primary Function** | Project Management | Email Marketing |
| **Client Portal Access** | Yes (in paid plans) | Limited |
| **Automation** | Task workflows | Email sequences |
| **Analytics** | Project/task metrics | Campaign performance |
| **Ease of Setup** | Moderate (learning curve) | Very easy |
| **Best For** | Team coordination | Audience engagement |
| **Pricing Model** | Seat-based | Contact-based |

## When Coaches Need Monday.com

Use Monday.com if you answer "yes" to most of these:

- You work with multiple clients simultaneously and need to track their individual progress
- You have team members, virtual assistants, or contractors
- You need to set deadlines, dependencies, and accountability structures
- You want one centralized place to manage everything from client onboarding to delivery

For example, a life coach managing ten simultaneous clients can create a "Client Journey" board showing each client's modules, assignments, and completion status. Your coaching assistant can update progress in real-time, and clients can see their own portal without accessing other client information.

## When Coaches Need Mailchimp

Mailchimp becomes essential if you're:

- Building an email list of prospects or past clients
- Sending regular newsletters or value content
- Running automated welcome sequences for new subscribers
- Tracking open rates and click-through rates to improve messaging
- Wanting to segment audiences (e.g., past clients vs. interested prospects)

A business coach might use Mailchimp to send a weekly email series about building sustainable businesses, automatically nurturing leads until they're ready to book a session. The platform shows exactly who engaged, allowing for personalized follow-ups.

## The Real Scenario: Why You Likely Need Both

Here's the truth many coaches discover: Monday.com and Mailchimp aren't competitors—they complement each other.

Monday.com keeps your *internal operations* clean. Mailchimp keeps your *external relationships* warm.

Imagine this workflow:
1. A prospect signs up through your website
2. Mailchimp automatically sends them a welcome sequence and educational content
3. When they're ready to work with you, they book a session
4. You create their project in Monday.com to track their coaching journey
5. Mailchimp continues sending them relevant content (progress tips, exclusive insights)

Without Monday.com, you're managing client progress through scattered emails and spreadsheets. Without Mailchimp, you're manually emailing prospects one-by-one, wasting hours that could go toward actual coaching.

## Cost Considerations for Your Coaching Business

**Monday.com** pricing starts around $12/month per seat for basic features, scaling up significantly if you add team members. For a solo coach, the basic plan covers essentials. With a VA, you're looking at $24+ monthly just for the platform.

**Mailchimp** is free up to 500 contacts, then around $13/month for 1,000 contacts. It's extremely affordable for coaches building their email lists. Scaling to 5,000+ contacts might cost $50-100/month depending on features.

A solo coach can start with Mailchimp's free tier and Monday.com's basic plan for roughly $12-15 monthly combined.

## Final Recommendation

**For solo coaches just starting:** Begin with Mailchimp (free tier) to build your email list, and use a simple task management system (even Trello's free version) temporarily. Upgrade to Monday.com once you're managing multiple concurrent clients.

**For coaches with teams or scaling practices:** Invest in both. The $25-50 monthly investment pays for itself through better client management and higher conversion rates from email nurturing.

**If you absolutely had to choose one:** Mailchimp. Email marketing directly generates revenue through bookings and sales. Client management can temporarily happen in spreadsheets or even Google Docs, but losing email subscribers means losing your audience.

The bottom line? Most successful coaches eventually use both tools together. They're not alternatives—they're complementary tools that create a complete system for managing clients and growing your audience. Start with what solves your most pressing pain point first, then add the other when you're ready to scale.
