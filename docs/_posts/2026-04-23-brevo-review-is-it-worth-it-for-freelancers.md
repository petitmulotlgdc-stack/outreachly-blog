# [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com/?via=outreachly) Review: Is It Worth It for Freelancers and Coaches?

If you're running a coaching business, consulting practice, or freelance operation, email marketing is non-negotiable. But with countless platforms out there, finding one that doesn't drain your budget while delivering results is challenging. Brevo has become increasingly popular among solopreneurs and small business owners. But is it actually worth your time and money?

In this Brevo review, we'll break down exactly what this platform offers, who it's best for, and whether it should be part of your business toolkit.

## What Is Brevo and How Does It Work?

Brevo is an all-in-one customer relationship management (CRM) and email marketing platform designed for small to medium-sized businesses. Originally known as Sendinblue, Brevo combines email marketing, SMS messaging, chat functionality, and CRM tools in a single dashboard.

For freelancers and coaches, the appeal is clear: you get multiple communication channels without juggling five different subscriptions. Whether you're nurturing leads, sending client updates, or following up on proposals, everything lives in one place.

The platform uses a freemium model, which means you can start at zero cost and upgrade as your needs grow. This makes it particularly attractive for bootstrapped coaches and consultants just starting their digital marketing journey.

## Key Features for Freelancers and Coaches

### Email Marketing Automation

Brevo excels at automation. You can create welcome sequences, abandoned cart reminders, or client follow-up workflows without manual intervention. For coaches offering multiple services or consultants managing several client types, automation saves hours every week.

The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, and templates are responsive across devices. You're not locked into basic designs—there's room to customize for your brand.

### SMS and Chat Integration

Unlike email-only platforms, Brevo lets you send SMS campaigns and use live chat on your website. For service-based businesses, this multi-channel approach means you can reach clients wherever they prefer to communicate.

Coaches receiving SMS reminders about upcoming sessions see higher attendance rates. Consultants using chat for quick questions reduce email clutter and improve response times.

### CRM Capabilities

The built-in CRM lets you track every interaction with prospects and clients. You can segment contacts based on behavior, create custom fields for your specific business, and see a complete communication history without switching tabs.

This is invaluable if you juggle multiple clients with different needs or manage a sales pipeline.

## Brevo Pricing: Breaking Down the Cost

Here's where Brevo gets interesting for budget-conscious professionals:

**Free Plan:**
- Up to 300 contacts
- Unlimited emails
- Basic automation
- No daily sending limits

**Premium Plans** start at around $20/month and scale based on contact list size. You pay for contacts, not volume sent, which rewards efficiency.

For freelancers with under 300 contacts, the free plan might genuinely work. For growing coaching practices or agencies, premium pricing remains competitive compared to [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/?via=outreachly) or ConvertKit.

## Pros and Cons for Freelancers and Coaches

### Advantages

**Affordable at scale.** You're not penalized for sending thousands of emails if you have a small contact list. This benefits coaches with engaged audiences.

**All-in-one solution.** Email, SMS, chat, and CRM eliminate tool-switching fatigue and reduce monthly subscriptions.

**Solid automation.** Workflows rival tools costing 2-3x more, making Brevo excellent value.

**Good customer support.** The documentation is thorough, and the support team is responsive—crucial when you're handling client communications.

### Disadvantages

**Learning curve.** New users sometimes find the interface cluttered compared to specialized platforms. The feature abundance can feel overwhelming initially.

**Limited advanced segmentation.** While basic segmentation works fine, sophisticated behavioral triggers require some technical knowledge.

**Modest template library.** Designs are functional but less visually stunning than competitors like Convertkit. You'll likely customize heavily.

**Reporting could be deeper.** Analytics exist but lack the granularity some data-driven coaches want.

## Is Brevo Worth It for Your Freelance Business?

**Choose Brevo if you:**
- Want multiple communication channels (email + SMS + chat)
- Need a CRM without buying separate software
- Have a growing contact list and want predictable pricing
- Value automation but don't need advanced AI features
- Work with a tight budget initially

**Look elsewhere if you:**
- Primarily need beautiful email templates for aesthetic brands
- Want best-in-class features for a single channel
- Require enterprise-level support and advanced analytics

## Final Recommendation

For most freelancers, coaches, and consultants, Brevo represents solid value. The free plan alone is generous enough to get started, and the paid tiers remain affordable as you scale. The multi-channel approach—combining email, SMS, and CRM—eliminates the need for multiple subscriptions that quickly become expensive.

The real win is simplicity. Managing client relationships, automating follow-ups, and reaching prospects through their preferred channels shouldn't require a business degree. Brevo delivers this without breaking the bank.

If you're currently juggling Mailchimp, Twilio, and a spreadsheet for client management, consolidating into Brevo will save time and money. Start with the free plan, test the features with your own audience, and upgrade when you're ready to scale.

Your freelance business deserves tools that work as hard as you do—and Brevo definitely qualifies.
