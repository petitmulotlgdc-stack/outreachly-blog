# [Calendly](https://calendly.com/?via=outreachly) vs Acuity for Small Business Owners: Which Scheduling Tool Wins?

If you're running a coaching practice, consulting business, or any service-based venture, you know how much time scheduling takes. Back-and-forth emails about meeting times drain your energy and lose you potential clients. The solution? A scheduling automation tool.

But with so many options available, how do you know which one is right for your business? Two names keep popping up: Calendly and Acuity Scheduling. Both promise to simplify your booking process, but they serve different needs.

In this guide, I'll break down the key differences between Calendly and Acuity so you can make an informed decision for your small business.

## Key Differences at a Glance

Before diving deep, let's establish what these tools actually do. Both Calendly and Acuity handle appointment scheduling, but they take different approaches.

Calendly focuses on simplicity and ease of use. It's the tool you set up in 10 minutes and immediately start sharing with clients. Acuity, on the other hand, is a more comprehensive business management platform that handles scheduling plus invoicing, payments, and client management.

Think of Calendly as a specialist and Acuity as a generalist. Your choice depends on whether you want one tool that does scheduling exceptionally well, or a more integrated platform.

## Ease of Setup and User Experience

**Calendly** wins decisively in this category. You can create your first scheduling link within minutes. The interface is clean and intuitive—even your tech-skeptical clients won't struggle with it.

With Calendly, you connect your calendar (Google, Outlook, iCal), set your availability, and share a link. Done. No complicated settings or learning curves.

**Acuity** requires more setup time. You'll spend 30-60 minutes configuring your business details, customizing forms, and setting up payment processing. However, this upfront investment pays off if you need these features.

For solopreneurs and new coaches, Calendly's simplicity is a major advantage. For established consultants who already manage complex workflows, Acuity's comprehensiveness may feel worth the extra setup.

## Pricing: Where Your Budget Matters

**Calendly** offers a genuinely free tier that covers basic scheduling needs. If you have a small client base, you might never need to upgrade. Paid plans start around $12/month for professionals and $20/month for teams.

**Acuity** also has a free version, but it's more limited. Most coaches and consultants need at least the Lite plan ($15/month) or Professional plan ($25/month) to unlock the scheduling features they need.

Here's the practical reality: if all you need is scheduling, Calendly's free plan might suffice indefinitely. If you need invoicing, advanced payment processing, or client relationship management, Acuity's investment becomes justified because you're replacing multiple tools.

## Best For: Coaching and Consulting Scenarios

**Choose Calendly if you:**
- Are just starting your coaching or consulting business
- Primarily need appointment scheduling
- Work with clients in different time zones
- Want minimal setup and maximum simplicity
- Don't need invoicing or advanced payment features
- Prefer a tool with excellent mobile experience

**Choose Acuity if you:**
- Have an established practice with recurring clients
- Need to collect detailed intake forms before appointments
- Want built-in invoicing and client payment history
- Manage a small team or have multiple service offerings
- Offer group coaching or workshops
- Need advanced email automation and client follow-up

## Integration Capabilities

Both tools integrate with popular platforms like Zapier, Gmail, Slack, and others. Calendly integrates with roughly 50 apps, while Acuity integrates with over 100.

For most small business owners, both options offer sufficient integration possibilities. If you use specialized coaching software (like Kajabi or Teachable), check compatibility before deciding.

## The Real Deciding Factor

Your decision ultimately comes down to this question: **Do you need more than scheduling?**

If your answer is no, Calendly is the clear winner. It's faster to set up, easier to use, more affordable, and does one thing exceptionally well.

If your answer is yes—you need invoicing, advanced forms, client management, or team coordination—Acuity becomes the better investment. You're essentially getting a lightweight business management platform built around scheduling.

## Final Recommendation

For most coaches, consultants, and small business owners just starting out: **begin with Calendly**. The free version is genuinely useful, setup takes minutes, and you'll immediately see the time savings from automation.

As your practice grows and you need invoicing, advanced client management, or team coordination, you can migrate to Acuity. Many successful practitioners do exactly this.

The worst choice? Overthinking this decision. Either tool will improve your business workflow dramatically compared to email scheduling. Pick one, implement it this week, and focus your energy on serving clients—not scheduling logistics.
