# [GoHighLevel](https://www.gohighlevel.com/?via=outreachly) vs Acuity for Coaches: Which Platform Wins?

Running a coaching business means juggling multiple responsibilities: client scheduling, payment processing, email marketing, and course delivery. Choosing the right software can mean the difference between spending 10 hours a week on admin tasks versus just 2.

If you're caught between GoHighLevel and Acuity, you're asking the right question. Both platforms promise to streamline your coaching business, but they take different approaches. Let's break down what each offers so you can make an informed decision.

## Understanding the Core Differences

GoHighLevel positions itself as an all-in-one business operating system. Acuity, owned by Squarespace, focuses primarily on scheduling and client management with some marketing automation built in.

This fundamental difference shapes everything else. GoHighLevel tries to be your entire backend. Acuity does fewer things but typically does them very well.

## Scheduling and Calendar Management

Both platforms excel at appointment scheduling, but with different strengths.

**Acuity's Strengths:**
- Intuitive calendar interface that feels natural immediately
- Powerful client intake forms that reduce back-and-forth emails
- Automatic reminders that actually reduce no-shows
- Time zone detection for clients across regions
- Beautiful scheduling page that matches your brand

**GoHighLevel's Strengths:**
- Calendar syncing with Google and Outlook
- Group scheduling and class management
- Custom booking pages with lead capture
- Pipeline management integrated with scheduling
- Automated follow-ups based on booking behavior

For coaches specifically, Acuity's intake forms are game-changers. You can collect health history, goals, and preferences before the first call, making sessions far more productive.

## Payment Processing and Invoicing

Money matters. Here's how they compare.

Acuity integrates with Stripe and PayPal, allowing clients to pay upfront or set up payment plans. The payment experience is smooth, and invoicing is straightforward.

GoHighLevel also handles payments through Stripe and offers something powerful: built-in recurring billing. If you run a monthly group coaching program, GoHighLevel's subscription management is more sophisticated. You get dunning management, retry logic for failed payments, and subscription analytics.

For most solo coaches, Acuity's payment system is sufficient. For those building membership or subscription revenue, GoHighLevel has the edge.

## Email Marketing and Automation

This is where GoHighLevel's all-in-one approach shines.

GoHighLevel includes a complete email marketing platform with automation workflows. You can create multi-step sequences, segment audiences, and track opens and clicks. SMS marketing is also included, which Acuity doesn't offer natively.

Acuity relies on third-party integrations with services like [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/?via=outreachly) or ConvertKit. This isn't necessarily bad—these platforms often do email better than built-in solutions. But it means more subscriptions and manual setup.

**The practical reality:** If you're comfortable integrating tools, Acuity plus a dedicated email platform gives you better email control. If you want everything together, GoHighLevel delivers convenience.

## Which Platform is Right for You?

**Choose Acuity if you:**
- Want the simplest scheduling interface possible
- Prefer integrating specialized tools for email and marketing
- Have a limited budget starting out
- Run a mostly one-on-one coaching business
- Value beautiful, client-facing design

**Choose GoHighLevel if you:**
- Want one platform to manage your entire business
- Run group programs, courses, or memberships
- Need SMS marketing alongside email
- Want built-in landing pages and funnels
- Plan to scale and add multiple revenue streams

## The Verdict

For solo coaches just starting out, **Acuity is the better choice**. It's cheaper, faster to implement, and handles scheduling beautifully. You can add email marketing and other tools as you grow.

For coaches scaling to multiple offerings, group programs, or who want to minimize tool sprawl, **GoHighLevel** makes more sense. Yes, it costs more, but the all-in-one ecosystem reduces friction and integrations headaches.

The best platform is the one you'll actually use consistently. Try both free trials, book a test appointment on each, and see which feels more natural. Your comfort with the interface matters more than feature lists.
