# [Brevo](https://www.brevo.com/?via=outreachly) Review: Is It Worth It for Coaches? Complete Guide for 2024

If you're a coach juggling multiple clients, handling email campaigns, and trying to nurture leads without breaking your budget, you've probably wondered whether Brevo is the right choice for your business. With so many email marketing platforms out there, it's hard to know which one actually delivers results without eating into your profits.

The truth? Brevo has become increasingly popular among coaches and consultants—but it's not a one-size-fits-all solution. Let me break down everything you need to know to make an informed decision.

## What Is Brevo and Why Should Coaches Care?

Brevo is an all-in-one customer relationship management (CRM) and email marketing platform designed to help small businesses and entrepreneurs manage their communications. For coaches specifically, it offers email marketing, SMS messaging, live chat, and CRM features—essentially everything you need to stay connected with clients and leads.

The platform positions itself as an affordable alternative to more expensive tools, which appeals directly to coaches who are bootstrapping their businesses or scaling up gradually.

## Key Features That Matter for Coaches

**Email Marketing Automation**
Brevo's automation allows you to create welcome sequences, follow-up campaigns, and client nurture emails without manual effort. You can segment your audience (new leads, active clients, past clients) and send targeted messages. For coaches, this means you can automatically follow up with prospects who downloaded your lead magnet without spending hours on repetitive emails.

**CRM Functionality**
The integrated CRM lets you track client interactions, store contact information, and see your entire communication history in one place. This is valuable when you're managing multiple coaching relationships and need to remember specific details about each client's goals and progress.

**SMS Marketing**
You can send text messages directly to clients—perfect for appointment reminders, quick motivational messages, or urgent updates. For coaches offering services, SMS can increase no-shows and keep clients engaged between sessions.

**Free Plan Option**
This is a major selling point. Brevo offers a genuinely useful free tier with unlimited contacts and up to 300 emails per day. For coaches just starting out or testing the platform, this is significant.

## Pricing Breakdown

Brevo's pricing is straightforward:

- **Free Plan**: Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day, basic automation
- **Starter**: €20/month - 10,000 contacts, advanced automation
- **Business**: €300/month - unlimited contacts, full feature access

For most coaches, the free plan or Starter tier makes sense. You're not forced into expensive packages, which is refreshing compared to competitors charging $50+ for entry-level plans.

## Pros: Why Coaches Actually Use Brevo

**Affordable scaling** – You only pay for what you need, and the free plan is genuinely functional.

**Combined toolset** – Email, SMS, and CRM in one place means less jumping between platforms and stronger data integration.

**Good automation workflows** – Building sequences is intuitive, and you can create sophisticated automation without technical skills.

**Decent deliverability** – Emails actually land in inboxes most of the time, which isn't guaranteed with all platforms.

**Live chat integration** – You can engage website visitors in real-time, helpful for coaches who want to answer questions immediately.

## Cons: Where Brevo Falls Short

**Learning curve** – The interface feels less polished than [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/?via=outreachly) or ConvertKit. It takes time to find features, and the organization could be cleaner.

**Template quality** – Email templates are functional but aesthetically basic compared to some competitors. You'll likely want to customize heavily.

**Limited advanced features** – If you need sophisticated behavioral triggers, advanced segmentation, or complex workflows, you'll outgrow Brevo relatively quickly.

**Customer support** – Brevo's support is slower than premium competitors. Responses can take 24-48 hours, which isn't ideal when you need quick help.

**Mobile app limitations** – The mobile experience is clunky compared to native apps from other platforms.

## Who Should Use Brevo?

Brevo works best for coaches who:
- Are budget-conscious and need multiple tools (CRM + email + SMS)
- Want to automate client communications
- Don't need bleeding-edge design or advanced features
- Are comfortable spending time learning a platform
- Want SMS and email in one system

Brevo isn't ideal if you:
- Prioritize beautiful email templates
- Need white-glove customer support
- Require advanced behavioral automation
- Want the simplest possible interface

## The Bottom Line: Is Brevo Worth It for Coaches?

**Yes—with conditions.** Brevo offers excellent value for coaches looking to combine email, SMS, and CRM functionality without significant expense. The free plan alone is worth exploring before committing money elsewhere.

However, it's not the most intuitive platform, and you'll need patience to set everything up. If you're willing to invest a few hours learning the system, the payoff—automated client communication, better organization, and multi-channel engagement—justifies the effort.

Start with the free plan. Build your email list, test automation, and see if it fits your workflow. If it does, upgrading to the Starter plan is a no-brainer investment in your coaching business.
